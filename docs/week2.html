2  Large sample theory

2.1 Convergence

The maximum likelihood estimator \(\hat\theta_n\) is distributed \(N(\theta, (nI_\theta)^{-1})\) for every \(\theta\), for large n.

An estimator \(\pmb{\hat \theta}\) of \(\pmb \theta\) is weakly consistent if the estimator \(\pmb{\hat \theta}\) converges in probability to the true value \(\pmb \theta\). An estimator \(\pmb{\hat \theta}\) of \(\pmb \theta\) is strongly consistent if the estimator \(\pmb{\hat \theta}\) converges with probability one to the true value (i.e., converges almost surely to \(\pmb \theta\)). An estimator \(\pmb{\hat \theta}\) is asymptotically normal if \(\sqrt n(\pmb{\hat \theta} - \pmb\theta)\) converges in distribution to a normal distribution with mean \(\pmb 0\) and covariance matrix \(\pmb \Sigma\).

\[ \begin{split} \text{Weak consistency: }& \pmb{\hat \theta} \xrightarrow{P} \pmb\theta \text{ as }n \rightarrow \infty \\ \text{Strong consistency: }& \text{ For any } \epsilon > 1, P(\lim_{n \rightarrow \infty}|\pmb{\hat \theta} - \pmb\theta| < \epsilon) = 1. \text{ That is,}\\ &\pmb{\hat \theta} \xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}} \pmb\theta\\ \text{Asymptotic normality: }& \sqrt n(\pmb{\hat \theta} - \pmb\theta) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, \pmb\Sigma) \end{split} \]

2.2 Central limit theorem (CLT)

Let \(X_1, X_2, ...\) be a sequence of iid random variables with \(E(X_i) = \mu\) and \(Var(X_i) = \sigma^2\). Then

\[ \begin{split} \frac{\sqrt n (\bar X_n - \mu)}{\sigma} &\xrightarrow{D} N(0,1)\\ \frac{\sqrt n (\bar X_n - \mu)}{\sigma} &\approx N(0,1) \\ \sqrt n (\bar X_n - \mu) &\xrightarrow{D} N(0, \sigma^2) \end{split} \]

2.3 Delta method

\[ \begin{split} \text{Let } X_n &\text{ be a sequence of RVs s.t.} \\ \sqrt n(X_n - \theta) &\xrightarrow{D} N(0, \sigma^2) \\ &\text{Then, } \\ \sqrt n\Big(g(X_n) - g(\theta)\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \sigma^2\Big[g'(\theta)\Big]^2\Big) \end{split} \]

Some useful identities to obtain \(N(0,1)\):

\[ \begin{split} N(0, k) &= \sqrt k N(0,1) \\ N(0, k^2) &= kN(0,1) \\ N(\mu, k^2) &= kN(\frac{\mu}{k}, 1) \end{split} \]

Consider iid random observations \(X_1, X_2,...\) with \(E(X_i) = \mu\) and \(Var(X_i) = \sigma^2 < \infty\). We want to estimate \(1/\mu\). Use the Delta Method to obtain the asymptotic distribution of \(\sqrt n\Big(\frac{1}{\bar X_n} - \frac{1}{\mu}\Big)\). We have (from the CLT):

\[ \begin{split} \sqrt n\Big({\bar X_n} - {\mu}\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} N(0, \sigma^2) \\ \sqrt n\Big(g(X_n) - g(\theta)\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \sigma^2\Big[g'(\theta)\Big]^2\Big) \\ \text{where } g(\theta) = \frac{1}{\theta},~ g'(\theta) &= -\frac{1}{\theta^2}, g'(\theta)^2 = \frac{1}{\theta^4}, \text{ and } \theta = \mu \\ \sqrt n\Big(\frac{1}{\bar X_n} - \frac{1}{\mu}\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \frac{\sigma^2}{\mu^4}\Big) \\ \sqrt n\Big(\frac{1}{\bar X_n} - \frac{1}{\mu}\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} \frac{\sigma}{\mu^2}N\Big(0, 1\Big) \\ \text{Using Slutsky's} &\text{ lemma}, \\ \frac{\sqrt n\Big(\frac{1}{\bar X_n} - \frac{1}{\mu}\Big)}{\sigma / \mu^2} &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, 1\Big) \\ \text{Plugging in consistent} &\text{ estimators}, \\ \frac{\sqrt n\Big(\frac{1}{\bar X_n} - \frac{1}{\mu}\Big)}{\sqrt{S^2_n} / \bar X^2_n} &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, 1\Big) \end{split} \]

2.4 Variance stabilizing transformations

We have \(X_1, X_2, ...\) iid Poisson(\(\lambda\)) random variables. Find an appropriate variance stabilizing transformation. We have

\[ \begin{split} E[X_i] = \text{Var}[X_i] &= \lambda. \text{From the CLT, } \\ \sqrt n (\bar X_n - \lambda) &\xrightarrow{D}N(0,\lambda) \\ \text{We wish to find a function } &g(\lambda) \text{ such that} \\ \lambda\Big[g'(\lambda)\Big]^2 &= c. \\ \text{A great candidate would be } c &= 1, \text{such that} \\ [g'(\lambda)\Big]^2 &= \frac{1}{\lambda} \\ g'(\lambda) &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{\lambda}} = \lambda^{-1/2} \\ g(\lambda) &= \int \lambda^{-1/2} ~d\lambda \\ &= \frac{1}{1/2} \lambda^{1/2} \\ &= 2\sqrt\lambda \\ \text{Using the Delta method to } &\text{confirm, we have} \\ \sqrt n\Big(g(X_n) - g(\lambda)\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \lambda\Big[g'(\lambda)\Big]^2\Big) \\ \sqrt n\Big(2\sqrt{X_n} - 2\sqrt{\lambda}\Big) &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \lambda\Big[g'(\lambda)\Big]^2\Big) \\ &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \lambda\Big[\frac{1}{\sqrt \lambda}\Big]^2\Big) \\ &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, \lambda\frac{1}{\lambda}\Big) \\ &\xrightarrow{D} N\Big(0, 1\Big) \\ \end{split} \]
